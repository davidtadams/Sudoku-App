Instructions

Welcome to my Sudoku Game!

Sudoku Rules

  • Every board has 9 regions, each region has 9 cells
  • Each individual region must contain the numbers 1-9
  • Each row on the board must contain the numbers 1-9
  • Each column on the board must also contain the numbers 1-9
  • Thus, the board can only contain the number 1 through 9
  • Pretty simple right? Play on!

Navigating the page

  • You can navigate around the board with your arrow keys or click on a cell to select it. Tip: You normally have to click on a cell on the board before you can use the arrow keys.
  • In order to enter in a number, all you have to do is type that number on your keyboad.
  • If you enter a number by mistake or want to change a number, then you can hit the Backspace, Escape, Delete, 0, or Space keys in order to delete that number. Just navigate to the cell you want to delete and then delete it.
  • There is a timer that automatically starts for you. If the timer makes you too anxious, you can just click on it and it will stop.

Features

  • By default, an easy puzzle is loaded. In order to change puzzle difficulties, use the top easy, medium, and hard buttons.
  • At any time, you can click the view solution button to view the solution for the current puzzle.
  • If you made a mistake or just want to start over, you can click the reset board button. WARNING! This will clear the board and delete your progress. If you just made one mistake, remember that you can just delete that number, you don't need to reset the board.
  • If you want to save a game and resume it later, you can click the save game button. Then, when you come back to the site, you will see a prompt asking you if you want to resume your game. If you want to resume it, just click yes at that prompt.
  • If you have no intention of resuming your saved game and want that prompt to go away, just click delete game.
  • If you want to try out a new puzzle of the same difficulty that you are currently on, you can click new puzzle.
  • At any point during your game, you can switch on live error checking. This will highlight any errors that you make or have made with a red box. You can just leave it on or use it to periodically check your status.
  • When you complete a puzzle, an alert will tell you whether or not your solution is correct.
  • All of the alerts that show up in the site will disable after a couple of seconds.
  • The number status bar under the board will change colors for numbers that have been completed in the puzzle. Meaning there are no more of those numbers to enter.
  • All of the puzzles are randomly pulled from an API that I created.
